Ontology Specification Draft

Abstract

The DIAMOND Project Ontology provides a comprehensive semantic framework for representing the organizational structure, competences, resources, and outputs of the DIAMOND (DIgital Absolute MAterials simulatioN Database) project, part of the French PEPR DIADEM (Integrated Devices for Accelerating the Deployment of Emerging Materials)). This ontology captures the key entities and relationships within the project, including work packages, partner institutions, personnel, scientific competences, computational resources, software tools, and research products. It is designed to facilitate knowledge management, enable semantic queries about project activities and outputs, and provide a machine-readable representation of the project's structure and deliverables. The ontology supports the project's mission to develop open-access databases, containerized scientific software, automated workflows, and machine learning tools for materials science, while documenting the diverse expertise gathered across computational methods, data management, and artificial intelligence approaches in materials modeling. By linking people to their competences, work packages to their products, and resources to their applications, this ontology serves as a living knowledge base that maps the DIAMOND project's contributions to the materials science community.
